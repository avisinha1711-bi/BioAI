Cancer metastasis is the process by which cancer cells spread from their original location (the primary tumor) to other parts of the body, forming new tumors. These cancer cells travel through the bloodstream or lymph system, a journey known as the metastatic cascade. Metastatic cancer cells retain the characteristics of the original cancer, and metastatic cancers are named after their primary site (e.g., metastatic breast cancer). This process is the primary cause of cancer deaths and often defines advanced (Stage IV) cancer.
